Pedagogical friction in the age of generative AI.

A public doorway into Micah Miner's dissertation research on how generative AI reshapes academic work, symbolic production, and the forms of resistance that learning still requires.

micahminer.com dissertation page. resource hub. research media.

Research focus.

This dissertation examines the educational consequences of generative AI as a symbolic technology. The central concern is not simply whether students use AI, but what happens when systems can produce language, summaries, arguments, and performances that look like learning while bypassing parts of the cognitive and interpretive labor learning depends on.

The project connects media ecology, learning sciences, and the concept of tertiary algorithmicity to study how educators and learners understand, resist, adopt, or redesign around AI's friction-reducing affordances.

Core ideas.

  • Pedagogical friction as productive resistance in learning environments.
  • Tertiary algorithmicity as a condition of machine-generated symbolic production.
  • Noetic, rhetorical, existential, and infrastructural forms of friction.
  • Design choices that preserve learning rather than merely accelerate output.
